WILDFIRE

Its caused by humans or lightning and can happen at any place with large vegetation, forests or dry material.

Before

Develop escaape plan.
Gather emergency supplies.
Keep water on hand for thrist and small fires.

During

Evacuate immediately if told by authorities.
Call 911 if unable to evacuate,use lights to reveal location if needed.

After

Listen to the authorities for more information.
Avoid still hot debris or materials.
Send text messages instead of calls as phone may be busy.
Wtch for possible flash floods.

EARTHQUAKES

It is sudden, rapid shaking of ground under surface.
It can happen without warning and can cause further disasters like fires, landslides,etc.

Before

Practice Drop, Cover and Hold on maneuvers.
Secure heavy items in home.
Create family emergency plan and make supply kits.

During

Drop down to hands and knees.
If using wheelchair, lock wheels and remain seated.
Cover your head and neck with arms.
If study table/desk nearby then crawl underneath them.
Stay at one position untill earthquake stops.

After

Expect aftershocks.
Check your health and the health of nearby people.
Move away from damaged buildings.
If trapped, protect mouth/eyes and send text messages or make loud noises to alert others.

FLOODS

Temporary overflow of water onto land.
It is caused either by rain,snow,storms or dam overflows.
It can be slow or fast and further cause outrages,disrupt transportation, damage buildings and landslides.

Before

Know the risks of flooding in your area.
Moniter potential signs of possible flooding.
Learn/practice evacuation routes and shelter locations.
Build emergency supply kits.
Protect important documents in waterproof containers and also keep digital copies.

During

Go to safe location.
Evacuate the place when told by authorities.
Do not walk, swim or drive in flood water.
In a building try to reach the highest level(Do not stay in attics by any means).

After

Avoid driving, wear heavy gloves/boots during cleanup.
Be aware of risk of electrocution.

EXTREME HEAT

Period of high heat and humidity above 90 degrees fahrenheit for atleast 2 or 3 days.
Can occur suddenly without warnings.
Old people,children and overweight are at risk.
Humidity keeps on increasing thus increasing heat.

Before

Know where cool centers are located.
Keep hoom cool by using air conditioners, covering windows, weaterproof homes and using reflectors to reflect heat.

During

Never leave a child, adult, or an animal alone in car on a hot day.
Stay in places with air conditioners.
If outside the house then stay in shade and wear hats.
Wear loose, light-weighted and light-colored clothes.
Drink plenty of fluids.
Avoid high energy activities.

After

Check health of yourself and your family.
Be prepared for next extreme heat period.

LANDSLIDES

Moving of masses of rocks , earth or debris down the slope.
It can occur quickly and without much warning.
Most likely during intense rainfall, runoff, or rapid snowmelt.
Can be faster than the speed of a running person.
It can occur in any direction.

Before

Be alert ofevacuation warnings and locate shelters
Avoid building houses on/near slopes, mountain edges, drainage systems or natural erosion valleys.
Be familiar with the land near you
Recognize the warning signs:listen sounds of rushing water or mud,rumbling visual signs such as shift in the position of trees,fences or boundaries.

During

Lsten to authorities, heed all warnings/evacuations.
Stay alert and awake, dont sleep untill landslide stops.
Be aware that it would be too late to outrun a landslide, never cross water/mud flows.
Move uphill when in path of landslide.
Avoid river valleys and low-lying areas.

After

Help the authorities to clean up the roads.
Take precautions while cleaning the roads.

THUNDERSTORMS

Lightning is the leading cause of injury or death.
Thunderstorms are the storms which include winds over 50 MPH which causes hails, flash flooding and tornadoes.

Before

Know the areas uder the risk of thunderstorms.
Identify nearby, sturdy buildings close to work/home.
Cut down the trees in danger of falling on homes.
Use surge protectors, lightning rods and protection systems.

During

Go indoors in sturdy buildings(safest place).
Avoid running water or landlines.
Unplug electric appliances and devices.
If outside/ can;t go inside building get into grounded vehicle/car.
Avoid flooded roadways.

After

Listen to authorities for more information.
Watch out for fallen power lines or trees.
